[ 6] We use the distinction "concern" as a translation of the English term, which we feel is more appropriate for what we want to convey. In Spanish, we have the verb concernir, but we do not have the corresponding noun (which would be equivalent to something like "concernimiento," as when we say discernimiento from the verb discernir). We therefore use the term concern, alluding to that which concerns us and prompts us to action. It is, therefore, the recognition of a primary situation of dissatisfaction, of unease, from which we act.

Another possible term would be incumbencia, that which concerns us when we act.
